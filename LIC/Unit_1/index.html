Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp)

Q: What is an Op-Amp?

A: An Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp) is a high-gain voltage amplifier with differential inputs and a single-ended output. It is one of the most widely used components in analog electronics.

Q: How does an Op-Amp work?

A: Imagine you have a magic box 🎩 that listens to two sounds 🎶 (voltages) and then speaks louder 📢 based on the difference between them. This magic box is called an Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp)!

Q: What are the inputs and output of an Op-Amp?

A: An Op-Amp has two ears 👂👂 (inputs) and one mouth 🗣️ (output):

  • One ear is called the (+) Non-Inverting Input
  • The other ear is called the (-) Inverting Input
  • The mouth (Output) speaks out the difference between the sounds it hears!

Q: What happens when the inputs change?

A:

  • If the + input is louder, the Op-Amp shouts positive 🔊 (+V).
  • If the - input is louder, the Op-Amp shouts negative 🔊 (-V).
